Web2 vs Web3

Web2: Web2 is the internet we use every day. We can create content, chat with others and use apps, but most of our data is controlled by companies.

Web3: Web3 aims to give users more control. Instead of relying on one company, apps use blockchain so users can own their digital identity and assets.

Bitcoin vs Ethereum

Bitcoin: Bitcoin was mainly created to work as digital money. Its main goal is to let people send and receive value without a bank.

Ethereum: Ethereum does that too, but it can also run smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps). Because of this, Ethereum is used for many blockchain projects besides payments.

Public vs Private Keys

Public Key: A public key is like your wallet address. You can safely share it with others so they can send you cryptocurrency.

Private Key: A private key is like the secret key to your wallet. It proves that you own the wallet and lets you access your funds. It should never be shared with anyone.

Blockchain vs Databases

Databases: A traditional database is usually managed by one company or organization. It can add, edit, or delete data whenever needed.

Blockchain: A blockchain stores data in linked blocks that are shared across many computers. Once data is added, it is very difficult to change, making it more transparent and secure.
